# Amendments log

This file records every change to the registered plan ([`preregistration.md`](preregistration.md)) made **after** the Open Science Framework (OSF) registration, in service of the open-science commitments in §8 of the pre-registration.

The OSF registration is an immutable, timestamped snapshot of the *original plan*. The
**authoritative record of every post-registration amendment is this file plus the public git
history** (on `main`, force-push-protected) in the repository that is **linked from the accepted
OSF registration**: each commit is timestamped and tamper-evident, which establishes the order
of every decision relative to the data — and that is what keeps the work accountable. (OSF's native
registration-Update mechanism is unavailable for this Open-Ended Registration, so the linked
repository *is* the amendment record rather than a mere companion to it. A separate OSF
registration per amendment is optional and not required for confirmatory status.)

Each entry states:
- **What** changed (section + summary),
- **Why**,
- **Type** — one of:
  - **pre-data amendment** — made *before* any real target data was analyzed (e.g. from expert review). Remains **confirmatory**.
  - **post-data change** — made *after* real data was seen. Flagged **exploratory / post-hoc**; never presented as confirmatory.

- **Far-infrared (Herschel / JWST-MIRI).** Required to constrain the sub-50 K regime that WISE
  cannot reach. Two distinct, limited modes: (a) deep facilities (JWST/MIRI mid-IR; Herschel
  far-IR, archival/decommissioned) are **pointed**, so they characterise *individual* candidates
  but cannot give a blind *population* limit; (b) all-sky far-IR surveys that *could* be
  cross-matched (**AKARI-FIS 65–160 µm, IRAS**) are far too shallow (~Jy vs the µJy–mJy of WD
  dust) to detect WD-level excess. **Possible tractable step (noted 2026-06-01):** an
  AKARI-FIS/IRAS all-sky cross-match purely to *extend the f_max figure into the far-IR and
  demonstrate* (rather than assert) that the <50 K regime stays unconstrained even with all-sky
  far-IR. Modest value; do only if a reviewer presses. Genuine sub-50 K progress needs a deep
  far-IR *survey* facility that does not currently exist (SPICA cancelled; PRIMA only proposed).

| 2026-06-09 | post-data (methods refinement) | §5 (battery classification) | **Two candidate-independent battery refinements**, surfaced when the combined T0+T1 unblind produced 15 morphology-resolvable residuals (T0 alone had produced none). **(1) Depth→radius eclipsing-binary criterion:** a transit depth implies an occulter radius, depth = (R_occ/R_star)²; with R_star ≈ 0.7 R_sun a depth > 0.13 implies R_occ > ~2.5 R_Jupiter — larger than any planet or brown dwarf, i.e. a stellar companion. Such a transit is now classed `eclipsing_binary` (catches faint-companion EBs whose secondary/odd-even signatures are too shallow to trigger the existing tests). **(2) Noise-aware depth variability:** the per-epoch depth-CV test for disintegrating bodies is now evaluated against the *noise expectation* (per-epoch depth uncertainty ≈ scatter/√n_in-transit) — only an EXCESS beyond noise counts as genuine variability. The raw CV was inflated by photon noise on shallow real transits, spuriously diverting them out of `natural_planet`. Both are pure physics / noise-model statements with **no free parameter tuned to any candidate** (the 0.13 depth and 2.5σ excess follow from the radius relation and Gaussian noise). **Effect on the limit: none** — injection–recovery confirms a flat occulter still classes RESIDUAL and a variable-depth tail still classes RESIDUAL, so C_i(box), C_i(tail), the detection bars, and `f_max` are all unchanged. Only the *labelling* of real by-product candidates (EB vs planet vs residual) is refined; the 15 resolve to their natural classes. Documented and applied uniformly to T0 and T0+T1 (re-run from k04). | Post-data but candidate-independent and limit-neutral: physical/statistical criteria, validated by pre-data injection semantics, not tuned to the objects they reclassify. Flagged as a methods refinement. |

| 2026-06-10 | post-data (methods refinement) | §3.3/§5 (battery; C_i) | **Battery v3 — red-noise-aware depth variability + single asymmetry boundary.** Adversarial review (an automated Claude reviewer, run on the v2 draft) fetched the multi-sector data for the 7 v2 residuals and showed that the v2 depth-variability test over-triggered: its white-noise floor (scatter/√n) under-predicts TESS faint-star *correlated* noise, so a star's per-sector "depth" merely tracked its per-sector scatter (depth–scatter correlation up to 0.97–0.99 for the worst cases). **Fix (1):** the per-epoch in-transit depth scatter is now compared to the same scatter measured at *off-transit* phases (an empirical control that inherits the star's correlated/sector noise); only an EXCESS over that floor counts as genuine variability. **Fix (2):** the planet/disintegrating asymmetry cut is unified at a single 0.15 boundary, removing the [0.1, 0.15] dead zone that had manufactured a residual from a threshold seam.
